Guardar como PDF Hinduism Hinduism is the world’s third largest religion with approximately 1.1 billion followers worldwide. Download FREE teacher-made resources covering 'Hinduism' View FREE Resources Hinduism fast facts: Deity: Hindus are monotheistic, meaning they worship more than one god, but Brahman is believed to be the supreme deity. Holy book: The Vedas are the Hindu scriptures. Approximate worldwide followers: 1.1 billion people, or 15% of the world’s population Countries that are predominately Hindu (examples of): India and Nepal Will my child learn about Hinduism at school? There is no statutory (compulsory) national curriculum for religion in schools. This means that the amount your child explores Hinduism and other world religions will depend largely on the school they attend and its denomination (if any). The national curriculum does encourage schools to provide their students with the opportunity to explore world religions in a rewarding and enriching way. Your child may spend time learning about Hinduism and its beliefs and practices. This can be extremely beneficial from both a spiritual and historical perspective, as understanding a variety of beliefs and cultures can develop empathy and spiritual awareness in your child. Exploring world religions can be a fun and interesting way to discover new cultures with your child. It may help them develop empathy and understanding for others, as well as build their natural curiosity into the world around them.
