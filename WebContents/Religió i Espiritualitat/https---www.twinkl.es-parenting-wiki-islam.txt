Download FREE teacher-made resources covering 'Islam' View FREE Resources What is Islam? Islam is a major world religion, and its followers are called Muslims. Similar to many other religions, Islam is guided by a sacred scripture, the Quâ€™ran. Central to the Islamic faith are the five pillars, which hold significant importance for both adults and young followers, shaping their lives from an early age. The primary pillar asserts: "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad (PBUH) is his messenger." This declaration stands as the cornerstone of Islam, as belief in it is foundational; without acceptance, the other pillars lose their relevance. The second pillar emphasises prayer, requiring Muslims to engage in five daily prayer sessions. Before prayer, cleanliness is stressed, and the ritual involves kneeling, bowing, and reciting passages from the Qurâ€™an. Charitable giving to support the less fortunate constitutes the third pillar of Islam. While financial contributions are encouraged, young individuals might also be urged to assist in non-monetary ways. Fasting, the fourth pillar, serves as a means for Muslims to cultivate gratitude for their blessings and gain insight into the challenges faced by the less fortunate. The fifth and final pillar involves the pilgrimage to Mecca, the holiest city for Muslims. The annual Hajj is a special pilgrimage, while Umrah, a pilgrimage that can occur at any time, is also considered significant. Mecca holds unparalleled importance in the Islamic world, making the pilgrimage a central aspect of the faith. Learn more about Islam in this Islam PowerPoint. What are some facts about Islam? The name of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) can be spelt in lots of ways. This is because our alphabet uses different letters. Islamic art is colourful and exciting. Some art uses geometric patterns, vegetal patterns and calligraphy. There are four holy books in Islam: the Qur'an, Tawrat (Torah or the Law), Zabur (Psalms) Injil (the Gospel). The Qur'an was not revealed to Muhammad (PBUH) in one go but over 23 years. The Qur'an was first sent down to Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) when he was 40 years old. Food is an important theme in many world religions. Muslims fast during Ramadan, some Christians eat simple lunches or meals during Lent, while Jewish people follow kosher food rules. Hajj is an Islamic pilgrimage and the hair is usually cut very short or even shaved off towards the end of Hajj. The moon and star are linked to Islam and Ramadan. By tradition, Ramadan starts at the sighting of the crescent moon. In the Islamic creation story, God sent angels to earth to bring back seven kinds of earth of many colours.
