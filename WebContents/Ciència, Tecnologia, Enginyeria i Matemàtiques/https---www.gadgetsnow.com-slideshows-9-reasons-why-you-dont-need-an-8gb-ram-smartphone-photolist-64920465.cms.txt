More and more smartphone brands are looking at launching smartphones with 8GB of RAM. However, to justify the existence of 8GB RAM smartphones right now, brands are mostly offering exceptionally high internal storage of up to 256GB. Both the features—high RAM and high storage— are a luxury that most users can skip. Most buyers feel that higher numbers in spec-sheet assures the best. However, when it comes to ROI, while higher storage space might come handy, the benefits of higher RAM on Android smartphones are debatable. Here are nine reasons as to why a smartphone with 8GB of RAM is not for everyone and also why you don’t need it. 2 10 Just a 'show' for spec-sheet lovers Last year, smartphones with 6GB RAM claimed user’s attention and in 2018, the hype is all-round 8GB RAM phones. Many smartphone enthusiasts love to drool over spec-sheets and thus it’s mostly a clever marketing strategy to sell something you may not even need. 3 10 Average users do not need 8GB RAM in smartphones What will you do with your new smartphone? If the answer to this question is limited to music, movies, gaming, social media and internet browsing then you surely don’t need an 8GB RAM Android phone. For this, it is logical to think that the games would be optimised to run across a large range of devices with far lesser RAM.
